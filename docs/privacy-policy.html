Privacy Policy

Card Box  ·  Effective date: June 2026  ·  Last updated: June 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how Card Box ("we", "us", or "our") handles information when you use our mobile application. Card Box is a local-first card organiser for non-bank cards — loyalty, membership, library, ID, transit, gift, and visiting — designed for all ages, including children.

✓ We do NOT collect, store, or share any personal data

1. Information We Collect

We collect no personal information.

Card Box operates entirely on your device. The only data the app stores are:

  • Your card entries (name, barcode/QR payload, optional notes, and optional front/back photos) saved locally in an encrypted SQLite database on your device.
  • App preferences (theme, biometric/PIN lock, sort order, custom categories) saved locally on your device via Android's SharedPreferences storage.
  • The encryption key that protects the database above, held in Android's hardware-backed keystore.

This data never leaves your device. It is not transmitted to any server, cloud service, or third party.

2. Internet & Network Access

Card Box does not hold the Android INTERNET permission. The app cannot make any network request — for telemetry, crash reporting, advertising, analytics, sync, or any other purpose. The only Android permissions the app requests are CAMERA (for scanning barcodes/QR codes and capturing the front and back of a card), NFC (for reading contactless cards), and USE_BIOMETRIC (for the optional app lock).

3. Advertising

No ads

Card Box contains no advertisements of any kind. We do not integrate any advertising SDK, ad network, or monetisation framework.

4. Third-Party SDKs & Services

Card Box integrates the Flutter framework and the following on-device Flutter plugins:

  • flutter_secure_storage — stores the database encryption key in Android's hardware-backed keystore.
  • shared_preferences — stores app preferences.
  • mobile_scanner + Google ML Kit text recognition — on-device only. The camera frame is processed in memory and never leaves the device.
  • nfc_manager — reads contactless card identifiers via the device's NFC radio. The read is local and not transmitted.
  • Drift + sqlite3 — local database engine.

No analytics, crash-reporting, social-login, advertising, or any other third-party SDK that transmits data is included in the app.

  • We do not collect any personal information from any user, including children under 13.

  • We do not display advertisements.
  • We do not allow in-app purchases.
  • We do not include social features, chat, or user-generated content.
  • All content in Card Box is appropriate for all ages.

✓ COPPA Compliant — no data collected from any user

✓ GDPR Compliant — no personal data processed

8. Data Security

Because Card Box stores everything on your own device, your data is protected by your device's own security mechanisms (full-disk encryption, screen lock, etc.) plus Card Box's local protections:

  • The card database is stored in an encrypted SQLite file. The encryption key is held in Android's hardware-backed keystore (TEE/StrongBox where available).
  • An optional app lock (PIN or device biometric) is offered in Settings. When enabled, the app must be unlocked on resume.
  • An in-app Erase all data action in Settings permanently wipes the database and key material from the device.

We have no servers and therefore no server-side data that could be breached.

9. Data Retention & Deletion

Local data (your card entries and preferences) is retained on your device until you uninstall the app or use the in-app Erase all data action. Either action permanently removes all locally stored app data from your device. Encrypted backups you choose to export are your responsibility to keep or destroy.

Continued use of the app after any changes constitutes acceptance of the revised policy. Significant changes that affect children's data (if we ever begin collecting any) will be communicated via an in-app notice.
